# Reading Guide

This dossier is structured as one business book. Each chapter has a distinct purpose and should be read in sequence.

## How To Read This Dossier

1. Read the front matter to understand the business intent, scope, and canonical domain name.
2. Read `docs/02-business-context/` to understand why the domain exists, who it serves, what value is expected, and what business boundaries apply.
3. Read `docs/03-business-capabilities/` to understand what the business must be able to do within that scope.
4. Read `docs/04-business-behavior/` to understand how those capabilities appear in real business use cases and scenarios.
5. Read `docs/05-business-information/` to stabilize the meaning of the core business terms and relationships used across the dossier.
6. Read `docs/06-cross-artifact-alignment/` to confirm that the chapters remain consistent, complete, and traceable.

## Canonical Language

Use the following terms consistently across all chapters:

- **Operator**: the platform party responsible for managing administrator companies at portfolio level
- **Administrator company**: the organization responsible for managing one or more communities
- **Community**: the housing community or residential building-level entity being administered
- **Owner**: a person who owns one or more estates or units
- **Estate / unit**: the owned property through which an owner participates in a community
- **Administrator assignment**: the time-bound relationship that links a community to its active administrator company

## Editorial Boundaries

- Keep all chapter content at business level.
- Treat Polish legal and regulatory context as part of the business framing, not as implementation detail.
- Preserve the distinction between stable business capabilities, business behavior, and business vocabulary.
- Do not introduce alternate names for the same stakeholder or concept without explicitly normalizing them.
