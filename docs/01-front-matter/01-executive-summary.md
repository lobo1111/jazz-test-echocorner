# Executive Summary

EchoCorner is being framed as a business platform for **housing community administration and resident communication in Poland**. The platform must support three primary business parties: platform operators, administrator companies, and owners who hold one or more estates or units within residential communities.

The business problem is twofold. First, the market and legal model for Polish housing communities must be understood well enough to define the correct operating rules, obligations, and records. Second, the product must support the day-to-day relationship between communities, their current administrator companies, and owners who need reliable communication and service channels.

The dossier treats the following business outcomes as central:

- administrator companies can manage portfolios of communities with clear accountability
- each community has one active administrator company at a time, with historical changes preserved
- owners can receive community-specific announcements and participate in permitted discussion
- owners can raise support or maintenance matters to the responsible administrator and track progress
- platform operators can onboard, govern, and reassign administrator companies and communities

This dossier is intentionally business-focused. It defines the operating model, business capabilities, business behavior, and business vocabulary needed before architecture, solution design, or implementation choices are considered.
