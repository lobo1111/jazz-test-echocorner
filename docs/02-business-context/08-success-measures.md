# Success Measures

## Core Indicators

Business success in this domain should be recognized through indicators such as:

- the share of communities with a clearly defined active **Administrator assignment**
- the timeliness and completeness of owner-facing community communication
- the share of owner support matters that are routed to the responsible **Administrator company** without avoidable reassignment or dispute
- the continuity of essential records and accountability during administrator-company transitions
- the consistency with which stakeholder terminology, scope, and business rules are used across the dossier and later operating work

## Qualitative Indicators

Qualitative evidence of success should include:

- owners reporting clearer understanding of where to obtain information and raise matters
- administrator companies reporting clearer responsibility boundaries for communication and support
- operator stakeholders reporting improved visibility into onboarding, assignment, and reassignment decisions
- reduced ambiguity in discussions about who is accountable for a given community interaction or historical record

## Baselines And Directional Measures

Current baselines are not yet established in the available input. The next business step is to confirm, with the **Operator** and participating **Administrator companies**, which current-state measures already exist and which must be defined for the first time.

Where baselines are absent, the initial ownership should sit with the **Operator**, supported by business representatives from **Administrator companies**, because those parties govern the portfolio view and can determine which performance and continuity indicators are meaningful enough to track consistently.

## Leading And Lagging Signals

Useful leading signals include timely establishment of new **Administrator assignments**, prompt publication of community information, and early routing of owner matters to the correct administrator company. Useful lagging signals include lower rates of disputed responsibility, fewer continuity gaps during reassignment, and stronger confidence in historical records and communication traceability.
