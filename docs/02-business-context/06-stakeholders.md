# Stakeholders

## Operator

The **Operator** is the portfolio-governance party for the domain. Its interests are in controlling which **Administrator companies** participate, assigning and reassigning community responsibility in a governed way, and maintaining confidence that the operating model remains accountable and traceable across communities in the Polish administration setting.

## Administrator Company

An **Administrator company** is the organization responsible for managing one or more **Communities** during the period in which those communities are assigned to it. Its interests are in clear assignment of responsibility, manageable communication obligations, orderly handling of owner support matters, defensible records, and continuity when communities enter or leave its portfolio.

## Community

A **Community** is the housing-community entity whose administration, communication, and support interactions are being governed. Its interests are in continuity of administration, clear accountability, relevant communication to participating owners, and preservation of business records when administrator responsibility changes. In the Polish setting, the community must be treated as an ongoing governed body whose interests are not exhausted by the current administrator relationship.

## Owner

An **Owner** is the person whose participation in a **Community** arises through ownership of one or more **Estates / units**. The owner's interests are in receiving relevant information, understanding when participation is permitted, raising matters to the responsible administrator company, and seeing that issues affecting the community are handled responsibly and traceably.

## Stakeholder Boundary Notes

- **Estate / unit** is not a stakeholder group. It is the business anchor through which an **Owner** has standing within a **Community**, and it determines which communications, discussion rights, and support matters are relevant to that owner.
- **Administrator assignment** is not a stakeholder group. It is the governing business relationship that allocates responsibility between a **Community** and its active **Administrator company** over time, and it therefore shapes continuity, authority, and record-keeping expectations across the domain.
