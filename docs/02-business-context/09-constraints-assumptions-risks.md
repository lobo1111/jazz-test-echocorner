# Constraints, Assumptions, And Risks

## Business Constraints

The chapter is constrained by the need to stay aligned with Polish housing-community administration practices without yet having a fully confirmed legal interpretation for every obligation. It must therefore treat community communication, administrator responsibility, and business records as governed activities that may later need to withstand formal review, while still avoiding unsupported legal claims. It is also constrained by the core business invariants already established for the dossier, including one active **Administrator company** per **Community** at a time, operator governance over onboarding and reassignment, and owner participation that depends on owned **Estates / units**.

## Assumptions

This chapter currently assumes:

- the Polish operating context makes administrator accountability, communication traceability, and record retention materially important business concerns rather than optional operational preferences
- housing communities require a recognizable allocation of administrative responsibility, even if some legal details of that allocation still need confirmation
- owners should have controlled participation rights that are specific to the communities in which they have standing
- the active **Administrator company** carries the primary business responsibility for community communication and initial support handling during its assignment, even if later research narrows that rule
- administrator reassignment must preserve enough historical continuity for governance, review, and owner confidence, including continuity of records and open matters where relevant

## Risks

The main business risks are:

- the exact Polish legal obligations for notices, records, administrator duties, and community governance may prove broader or narrower than currently assumed
- the business boundary for owner-to-owner interaction may be misdrawn if later research shows the domain should permit more or less community discussion
- the distinction between community-wide matters and estate- or unit-specific matters may require a sharper support-handling boundary than is currently defined
- the distinction between **Estate** and **Unit** may remain ambiguous and create inconsistent stakeholder or eligibility interpretation
- reassignment between administrator companies may require more explicit continuity, transfer, access, or retention rules for announcements, comments, tickets, and other records than are currently understood
- the **Operator** role may carry stronger compliance or supervisory obligations than the current framing reflects

## Unresolved Framing Questions

The following questions should remain visible for later refinement:

- Which administration duties belong to the **Community** itself and which are delegated in practice to the active **Administrator company**?
- Which communication events in the Polish housing-community context are merely operational and which carry formal business or legal significance?
- Should support matters be understood primarily at community level, estate or unit level, or in a combination that depends on the case?
- What business records must remain visible, transferable, or auditable after an **Administrator assignment** ends?
- Are **Estate** and **Unit** interchangeable in this domain, or do they represent different business concepts with different implications?
