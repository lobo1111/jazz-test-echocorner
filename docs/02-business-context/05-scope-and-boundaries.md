# Scope And Boundaries

## In Scope

This chapter treats the following business areas as in scope:

- the Polish housing-community administration setting in which a **Community** is an ongoing administered body with owners, operating responsibilities, and communication obligations
- the relationship between an **Operator**, **Administrator companies**, **Communities**, and **Owners**, including ownership-based standing through **Estates / units**
- the rule that a **Community** has exactly one active **Administrator company** at a time
- the time-bound nature of an **Administrator assignment**, including reassignment, handover accountability, and historical continuity
- owner access to community-specific announcements, permitted discussion, and support interactions where participation depends on legitimate community standing
- support and maintenance matters routed to the administrator company responsible for the relevant community
- business records, traceability needs, and accountability concerns that arise from communication, support, and reassignment, especially where later review or legal challenge may depend on them

## Out Of Scope

The following business areas are outside the current dossier boundary unless later research proves they are essential to the core administration model:

- solution architecture, technical access-control design, tenancy design, or implementation mechanics
- screen design, user-interface flows, or interaction details
- detailed statutory analysis, court interpretation, or legal advice beyond the business framing needed to understand obligations and risks
- detailed accounting, billing, or payment administration
- full treatment of meeting governance, voting, or formal notice procedures unless later research shows they are inseparable from the confirmed administration scope
- unrestricted owner-to-owner social networking beyond the confirmed community communication scope
- broader facility operations that do not materially affect administrator accountability for community communication or support

## Adjacent Domains

Several adjacent areas may influence this domain without belonging fully inside it:

- Polish legal and regulatory interpretation, which shapes obligations, records, and accountability but is not yet fully confirmed
- property finance and formal community governance processes, which may become relevant if later research shows they are inseparable from administration duties
- physical service delivery by contractors or other third parties, where this dossier is concerned mainly with the administrator's responsibility to govern, communicate, and track matters rather than to perform specialist work directly

## Boundary Notes

Two boundaries require special care while the domain is still being clarified:

- the line between permitted community discussion and broader owner-to-owner interaction remains unresolved and must not be assumed too broadly
- the line between community-level support and estate- or unit-specific support also remains unresolved and must be made explicit in later chapters rather than silently fixed here
- the line between general community communication and communications that carry formal business or legal significance remains important and may require later expansion if Polish operating obligations prove broader than currently assumed
