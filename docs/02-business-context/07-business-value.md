# Business Value

## Value For Owners

The domain creates value for **Owners** by giving them dependable, community-specific access to information that affects their interests. It also gives them a recognized path to raise support or maintenance matters with the **Administrator company** responsible for the relevant **Community**, reducing uncertainty about where to turn and who is accountable.

## Value For Administrator Companies

The domain creates value for **Administrator companies** by clarifying which communities they are responsible for, what owner-facing communication obligations they carry, and how support matters should be managed within that responsibility. This supports more orderly portfolio management and reduces ambiguity when communities are reassigned.

## Value For The Operator

The domain creates value for the **Operator** by making administrator-company onboarding, portfolio governance, and reassignment part of a controlled business model rather than an informal coordination exercise. This improves oversight and strengthens the ability to govern accountability across multiple communities.

## Operational And Control Value

Beyond stakeholder-specific benefits, the domain creates broader operational and control value through clearer records, stronger continuity during administrator transitions, and more defensible handling of community communication and support interactions. In a Polish housing-community setting, that control value is especially important because legal or regulatory obligations may attach to who communicated what, who was responsible at the time, and which records must be retained.

## Strategic Value

Strategically, the initiative creates value by establishing a coherent business foundation for a market that depends on trust, continuity, and controlled participation. A stable business frame makes later product and process decisions easier because the core domain boundaries and business obligations are already explicit.
