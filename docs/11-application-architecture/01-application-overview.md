# Application Overview

## EchoCorner As One Functional System

From an application perspective, EchoCorner is a **single product** that helps an `Operator` govern administrator participation, helps an active `Administrator company` run community communication and accountable support, and preserves **explainable history** across administrator change.

The dossier stresses tensions that the application must keep visible in its internal shape:

- **Current responsibility** versus **historical continuity** — live assignment and case state must not be duplicated or contradicted by archival or reporting views.
- **Public community communication** versus **accountable support handling** — both use community and owner vocabulary, but they follow different lifecycles and accountability rules.
- **Policy variation** versus **stable boundaries** — eligibility, communication significance, and support granularity may evolve; the functional split should absorb change through policy before forcing structural churn.

## Main Internal Concerns

| Concern | Why it matters inside the application |
| --- | --- |
| **Authoritative assignment truth** | Reassignment and handover break if any internal area redefines who is currently responsible for a `Community`. |
| **Audience and participation rules** | Community-facing features depend on one coherent model of who may see and discuss what, tied to glossary concepts such as `Community membership eligibility`. |
| **Support matter lifecycle** | Owner-raised support must remain a distinct accountable thread, not folded into announcements or comments. |
| **Durable evidence** | The product’s value includes defensible records; internal collaboration must feed continuity without making the continuity area the runtime owner of active state. |
| **Shared policy and delivery** | Rules and notifications span blocks; their placement must avoid either scattering policy or centralizing decisions that belong with operational ownership. |

Chapter 08 named four **functional building blocks** that span these concerns. Chapter 11 refines how those blocks collaborate inside the application while preserving the same external accountability story the domain chapter describes.
