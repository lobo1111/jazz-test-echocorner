# Information Domain Overview

The information domain for **Housing Community Administration and Resident Communication in Poland** is built around accountable relationships rather than around isolated records. The key question is not only what information exists, but why the business needs that information to recognize responsibility, confirm participation rights, support communication, and preserve continuity when administrator responsibility changes.

## Why Information Concepts Matter Here

Information concepts matter because this domain depends on stable business meaning across several parties at once:

- the **Operator** governs which **Administrator companies** may participate and which communities they serve
- each **Community** must have exactly one active **Administrator company** at a time
- an **Owner** participates through owned **Estates / units**, not through general platform presence
- communication and support interactions create **Communication records** that may remain important after the active administrator changes

If these meanings are weak or inconsistent, the dossier cannot describe eligibility, accountability, reassignment, or record continuity coherently.

## Central Concept Groups

The chapter organizes the information landscape into four concept groups:

- **Governance parties:** `Operator`, `Administrator company`, and `Community` define who governs, who administers, and what is being administered.
- **Responsibility relationship:** `Administrator assignment` makes the time-bound community-to-administrator relationship explicit.
- **Participation anchors:** `Owner`, `Estate / unit`, and `Community membership eligibility` explain why a person may access or participate in community-specific communication.
- **Operational and evidentiary concepts:** `Community wall post`, `Support ticket`, and `Communication record` explain how business interactions are expressed and preserved.

## High-Level Vocabulary Framing

This chapter uses a narrow, business-facing vocabulary on purpose:

- use **Community** as the governed housing-community body, not as a casual social group
- use **Administrator company** as the accountable operating organization for a community during an assignment period
- use **Administrator assignment** to talk about the active or historical relationship between a community and its administrator company
- use **Community membership eligibility** to talk about business entitlement to participate in communication for a specific community
- use **Communication record** when continuity, traceability, or historical truth matters across announcements, comments, support matters, and administrator changes

Two vocabulary boundaries remain intentionally visible rather than normalized away:

- **Estate** and **Unit** may be synonyms or separate business concepts
- the exact boundary between operational communication, formal communication, and durable record obligations still needs business clarification

This chapter therefore aims to stabilize the strongest concepts first, while keeping unresolved semantics explicit for later resolution.
