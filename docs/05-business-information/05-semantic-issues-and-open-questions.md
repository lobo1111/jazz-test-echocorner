# Semantic Issues And Open Questions

This file keeps unresolved meaning issues explicit so later chapters do not answer them inconsistently. The business-context source of record remains `docs/02-business-context/09-constraints-assumptions-risks.md`; the notes below translate those risks into vocabulary guidance for this chapter.

## Estate Versus Unit

- **Current working stance:** use the paired term `Estate / unit` unless a source explicitly requires one term.
- **Why it is unresolved:** the dossier has not yet confirmed whether the terms are synonyms or distinct business concepts with different implications for standing and support scope.
- **Vocabulary effect:** concept definitions, glossary entries, and later chapters should avoid silently choosing one meaning.
- **Source reference:** `docs/02-business-context/09-constraints-assumptions-risks.md`

## Basis Of Community Membership Eligibility

- **Current working stance:** eligibility is tied to recognized owner standing in a specific community through an `Estate / unit`.
- **Why it is unresolved:** the dossier does not yet define the exact business evidence, review steps, or edge cases that activate, suspend, or end eligibility.
- **Vocabulary effect:** later chapters may describe eligibility as a business status, but should not imply a fully normalized rulebook.
- **Source reference:** `docs/02-business-context/09-constraints-assumptions-risks.md`

## Scope Of Permitted Discussion

- **Current working stance:** controlled discussion around community communication is in scope; unrestricted owner-to-owner social interaction is not assumed.
- **Why it is unresolved:** the business context leaves open whether discussion remains comment-based or expands into broader interaction.
- **Vocabulary effect:** `Community wall post` should remain the anchor concept for discussion until the business confirms a wider communication model.
- **Source reference:** `docs/02-business-context/05-scope-and-boundaries.md`, `docs/02-business-context/09-constraints-assumptions-risks.md`

## Support Ticket Granularity

- **Current working stance:** a `Support ticket` belongs to the accountable community-administration model, but it may be community-wide, estate-specific, or unit-specific depending on the matter.
- **Why it is unresolved:** the business has not finalized the correct primary frame for support responsibility and ticket interpretation.
- **Vocabulary effect:** later chapters should not assume that every ticket is purely community-level or purely unit-level.
- **Source reference:** `docs/02-business-context/05-scope-and-boundaries.md`, `docs/02-business-context/09-constraints-assumptions-risks.md`

## Communication Record Continuity Across Reassignment

- **Current working stance:** historical posts, comments, tickets, and related evidence remain part of the business story after administrator change.
- **Why it is unresolved:** the business context does not yet define which record types must be transferred, retained, shared, or remain visible to successor parties.
- **Vocabulary effect:** `Communication record` must stay broader than any one current administrator's working materials.
- **Source reference:** `docs/02-business-context/09-constraints-assumptions-risks.md`

## Formal Versus Operational Communication

- **Current working stance:** some community communication may carry stronger business or legal significance than routine updates.
- **Why it is unresolved:** the Polish operating context may impose obligations that differ by communication type, but that boundary is not yet normalized.
- **Vocabulary effect:** `Community wall post` should be used for the in-scope communication unit, while notes should flag where stronger formal meaning may apply.
- **Source reference:** `docs/02-business-context/05-scope-and-boundaries.md`, `docs/02-business-context/09-constraints-assumptions-risks.md`

## Operator Oversight Depth

- **Current working stance:** the `Operator` governs participation, assignment, and reassignment.
- **Why it is unresolved:** the dossier still leaves open whether the operator also carries ongoing compliance or supervisory responsibilities beyond portfolio governance.
- **Vocabulary effect:** later chapters should not overstate or understate the operator's continuing role without explicit confirmation.
- **Source reference:** `docs/02-business-context/09-constraints-assumptions-risks.md`
