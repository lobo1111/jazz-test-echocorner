# Administrator Company

## Definition

An **Administrator company** is the organization that manages one or more communities during the period in which those communities are assigned to it.

## Why It Matters

The administrator company is the main accountable operating party for community communication, initial support handling, and continuity during the time a community sits in its portfolio.

## Key Characteristics

- may manage multiple `Community` instances at the same time
- becomes responsible for a community only through an `Administrator assignment`
- carries day-to-day accountability for community-facing communication and support during the active assignment
- must preserve defensible records and support continuity when responsibility begins or ends

## Relationships To Other Concepts

- is governed by the `Operator`
- serves a `Community` through an `Administrator assignment`
- issues or maintains `Community wall post` content during the active assignment
- handles `Support ticket` matters for the communities currently assigned to it
- creates and stewards `Communication record` items relevant to its active and historical responsibilities

## Lifecycle States

Typical business states are:

- candidate for participation
- authorized participant in the governed portfolio
- actively assigned to one or more communities
- formerly assigned to a community but still linked to historical records

## Business Owner Or Steward

The organization itself stewards its active responsibilities. The `Operator` governs whether it may participate in the portfolio.

## Synonyms Or Alternate Names

- community administrator company
- administrator organization

## Rules And Constraints

- an administrator company is not responsible for a community unless an `Administrator assignment` makes that responsibility active
- responsibility for communication and support is community-specific, not global across all owners
- reassignment must not erase the business history created while the company was the active administrator
