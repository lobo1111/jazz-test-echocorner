# Support Ticket

## Definition

A **Support ticket** is an accountable business matter raised by an owner to the administrator company responsible for the relevant community.

## Why It Matters

The support ticket concept expresses how owners bring maintenance or support concerns into the governed administration model. It creates a business object around responsibility, progress, and outcome rather than leaving support as informal conversation.

## Key Characteristics

- originates from an `Owner` with standing in a `Community`
- is handled by the active `Administrator company` for that community
- tracks a matter that requires attention, follow-up, or resolution
- contributes to business accountability and traceability over time

## Relationships To Other Concepts

- is raised by an `Owner`
- is relevant to a `Community`
- may be contextualized by an `Estate / unit`
- is routed according to the active `Administrator assignment`
- becomes part of the durable `Communication record`

## Lifecycle States

Typical business states are:

- submitted
- acknowledged
- in handling
- resolved
- closed, with history retained

## Business Owner Or Steward

The active `Administrator company` is the operating steward of the ticket while the matter remains under its responsibility. The originating `Owner` remains the business party that raised the matter.

## Synonyms Or Alternate Names

- support matter
- service request

## Rules And Constraints

- a support ticket must be directed to the administrator company currently responsible for the relevant `Community`
- support accountability follows the active `Administrator assignment`, even when the matter later needs continuity across reassignment
- the business boundary between community-level and estate- or unit-specific tickets remains unresolved
