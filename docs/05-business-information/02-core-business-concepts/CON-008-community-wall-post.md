# Community Wall Post

## Definition

A **Community wall post** is a community-specific business communication item published for the owner audience of a given community by, or under the responsibility of, the currently accountable administrator company.

## Why It Matters

This concept gives the dossier a clear term for the core communication object around which announcements, updates, and controlled discussion can occur. It distinguishes governed community communication from general social posting.

## Key Characteristics

- belongs to one `Community`
- is attributable to the `Administrator company` responsible through the active `Administrator assignment`
- is intended for owners with `Community membership eligibility`
- may carry operational importance, and some posts may also carry stronger formal or evidentiary significance
- may attract governed comments or follow-up communication

## Relationships To Other Concepts

- is issued in the context of a specific `Community`
- is attributable to the active `Administrator company` through an `Administrator assignment`
- is visible or relevant to an `Owner` only where `Community membership eligibility` exists
- contributes to the wider `Communication record`

## Lifecycle States

Typical business states are:

- prepared for publication
- published and active
- superseded or no longer current
- retained as historical record

## Business Owner Or Steward

The active `Administrator company` is the usual steward while the assignment remains active. Historical stewardship and access after reassignment may involve shared responsibility with the `Operator`.

## Synonyms Or Alternate Names

- community announcement
- community notice
- wall communication

## Rules And Constraints

- a wall post is a governed community communication, not an unrestricted social post
- a post must remain attributable to the responsible administrator relationship in force when it was issued
- visibility and interaction around a post depend on `Community membership eligibility`
- posts with lasting business significance may need retention beyond the end of the active administrator relationship
