# Estate / Unit

## Definition

**Estate / unit** is the business property concept through which an owner's standing in a community is anchored. In the current dossier, the exact distinction between **estate** and **unit** remains unresolved, so the term is carried as a controlled paired expression.

## Why It Matters

This concept explains why a specific owner is connected to a specific community. Without it, community membership eligibility, support relevance, and the scope of owner participation would lack a business anchor.

## Key Characteristics

- links an `Owner` to a `Community`
- provides the basis for community-specific participation rights
- may influence whether a support matter is treated as community-wide, estate-specific, or unit-specific
- is a business anchor, not a stakeholder role

## Relationships To Other Concepts

- is held by an `Owner`
- is associated with a `Community`
- supports `Community membership eligibility`
- may provide context for a `Support ticket`

## Lifecycle States

Typical business states are:

- acquired or recognized as the basis of standing
- actively held
- transferred, sold, or otherwise no longer the basis of standing

## Business Owner Or Steward

The underlying evidence for this concept likely comes from ownership or administration records outside this chapter. The active `Administrator company` relies on that evidence when determining community-specific standing.

## Synonyms Or Alternate Names

- estate
- unit
- property holding

## Rules And Constraints

- do not assume that **estate** and **unit** are interchangeable until the business explicitly normalizes the terms
- an estate or unit is the basis of standing, not a stakeholder in its own right
- later chapters should use the paired term when ambiguity would otherwise create inconsistent meaning
