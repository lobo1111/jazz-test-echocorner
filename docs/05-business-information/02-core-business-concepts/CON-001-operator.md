# Operator

## Definition

The **Operator** is the portfolio-governance party that controls which administrator companies may participate in the domain and which communities they are allowed to serve.

## Why It Matters

The domain depends on one governing party to keep administrator participation, community assignment, and reassignment accountable at portfolio level. Without the Operator concept, it becomes unclear who authorizes administrator-company participation or who governs changes in community responsibility.

## Key Characteristics

- acts at portfolio level rather than at the level of one community
- governs administrator-company onboarding, assignment, and reassignment
- protects continuity and accountability when community responsibility changes
- may carry oversight or compliance duties broader than day-to-day communication handling

## Relationships To Other Concepts

- governs the participation of `Administrator company`
- authorizes or oversees `Administrator assignment`
- relies on `Communication record` when continuity, audit, or historical truth must be preserved

## Lifecycle States

The Operator is a stable governance role in this dossier. Its own lifecycle is less important than the decisions and oversight actions it performs over administrator-company participation and assignment changes.

## Business Owner Or Steward

The steward is typically the platform-owning governance organization.

## Synonyms Or Alternate Names

- platform operator
- portfolio operator

## Rules And Constraints

- the Operator does not replace the day-to-day accountability of the active `Administrator company`
- governance must preserve the rule that a `Community` has exactly one active `Administrator company` at a time
- the exact scope of ongoing compliance or supervisory duties remains an open business question
