# Community Membership Eligibility

## Definition

**Community membership eligibility** is the business standing that entitles a specific owner to access or participate in communication for a specific community.

## Why It Matters

This concept prevents community communication from being treated as general public interaction. It explains why an owner may view announcements, comment where permitted, or raise community-relevant matters, and it keeps participation tied to legitimate community standing.

## Key Characteristics

- is specific to one `Community`, not global across the whole portfolio
- is typically grounded in an `Owner`'s relationship to an `Estate / unit`
- may need to be recognized, maintained, changed, or ended over time
- affects who may receive `Community wall post` communication and who may raise a `Support ticket`

## Relationships To Other Concepts

- gives an `Owner` standing within a `Community`
- is usually justified by an `Estate / unit` relationship
- governs participation around `Community wall post` communication
- influences who may create or be linked to a `Support ticket`
- may itself become part of the `Communication record` when participation rights are challenged or changed

## Lifecycle States

Typical business states are:

- not yet recognized
- active
- suspended, disputed, or under review
- ended because the basis of standing no longer applies

## Business Owner Or Steward

The active `Administrator company` usually interprets and maintains this standing for operational purposes within a community. The underlying business basis may depend on ownership evidence or community rules that come from outside this chapter.

## Synonyms Or Alternate Names

- community standing
- participation eligibility
- owner entitlement

## Rules And Constraints

- eligibility is community-specific and must not be treated as a general platform right
- one `Owner` may have eligibility in multiple communities through multiple holdings
- the business may need different participation thresholds for reading, commenting, and raising support matters
- the exact evidence and decision rules for eligibility remain an open business question
