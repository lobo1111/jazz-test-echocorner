# Communication Record

## Definition

A **Communication record** is the durable business evidence that communication, participation, support, and responsibility events occurred in a specific community context and must remain available for continuity, accountability, or historical truth.

## Why It Matters

This concept prevents important communication and support history from being treated as disposable operational chatter. It gives the dossier a clear term for the evidence that must survive administrator change, dispute, review, or later business reference.

## Key Characteristics

- preserves business meaning over time rather than only the moment of interaction
- may include evidence connected to `Community wall post` items, comments, `Support ticket` exchanges, and assignment decisions
- remains relevant across changes in the active `Administrator assignment`
- supports review, audit, dispute handling, and continuity of community knowledge

## Relationships To Other Concepts

- preserves the history of `Administrator assignment` decisions and changes
- captures durable evidence linked to `Community wall post` communication
- captures durable evidence linked to `Support ticket` handling
- may evidence changes or disputes around `Community membership eligibility`
- is relied upon by the `Operator`, `Administrator company`, and `Community` when continuity matters

## Lifecycle States

Typical business states are:

- created as part of a business event
- actively relied upon for current operations or review
- retained as historical evidence
- eventually subject to disposal only when business or legal obligations allow

## Business Owner Or Steward

Stewardship is shared. The active `Administrator company` usually creates or maintains many records during its assignment, while the `Operator` may govern continuity expectations and retained historical truth across assignment changes.

## Synonyms Or Alternate Names

- business record
- communication evidence
- accountable history

## Rules And Constraints

- not every communication event necessarily has the same record significance, and the boundary between routine communication and formal record remains unresolved
- historical truth must not be erased when an `Administrator assignment` ends
- records may require different access, retention, or transfer treatment depending on their business significance
- record stewardship is a business obligation before it becomes a technical retention problem
