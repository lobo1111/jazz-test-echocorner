# Community

## Definition

A **Community** is the governed housing-community body whose administration, communication, and support matters are managed within this domain.

## Why It Matters

The community is the central business subject of the dossier. Administrator responsibility, owner participation, announcements, support matters, and record continuity all attach to a specific community context.

## Key Characteristics

- is an ongoing business body, not just a temporary audience or chat group
- must have exactly one active `Administrator company` at a time
- may experience changes in administrator responsibility over time
- has owners whose standing arises through owned `Estate / unit` relationships
- needs continuity of communication, support handling, and records beyond any single administrator relationship

## Relationships To Other Concepts

- is served through an `Administrator assignment`
- includes owners with `Community membership eligibility`
- contains `Community wall post` communications relevant to that community
- is the context for `Support ticket` matters raised to the responsible administrator company
- depends on `Communication record` continuity across assignment changes

## Lifecycle States

The concept is treated as persistent. What matters most is not the lifecycle of the community itself, but the lifecycle of its administrator relationships, participation rights, and business records over time.

## Business Owner Or Steward

The community remains the enduring business subject. The active `Administrator company` stewards many day-to-day responsibilities on its behalf during the assignment period.

## Synonyms Or Alternate Names

- housing community
- managed community

## Rules And Constraints

- a community must have one and only one active `Administrator company` at a time
- historical `Administrator assignment` relationships remain part of the community's business history
- community participation is limited to parties with legitimate standing rather than general public membership
