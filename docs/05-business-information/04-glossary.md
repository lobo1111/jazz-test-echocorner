# Glossary

This glossary consolidates the preferred business vocabulary for **Housing Community Administration and Resident Communication in Poland**. Where a fuller definition exists, the concept file is linked directly.

| Preferred term | Definition | Optional synonyms | Notes on usage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [`Operator`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-001-operator.md) | Portfolio-governance party that controls administrator-company participation and community responsibility. | Platform operator, portfolio operator | Use for the governing business role above individual communities. |
| [`Administrator company`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-002-administrator-company.md) | Organization that manages one or more communities during the time they are assigned to it. | Community administrator company, administrator organization | Use for the accountable operating party, not for the assignment itself. |
| [`Community`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-003-community.md) | Governed housing-community body whose communication and support interactions are in scope. | Housing community, managed community | Use as a business body, not as a casual group or chat audience. |
| [`Administrator assignment`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-004-administrator-assignment.md) | Time-bound relationship that makes one administrator company responsible for one community. | Community assignment, administrator relationship | Use when discussing current or historical responsibility over time. |
| [`Owner`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-005-owner.md) | Person whose standing in a community arises through owned estates or units. | Property owner, community owner | Participation is community-specific, not platform-wide. |
| [`Estate / unit`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-006-estate-unit.md) | Property concept that anchors an owner's standing within a community. | Estate, unit, property holding | Keep the paired term where the distinction between estate and unit is still unresolved. |
| [`Community membership eligibility`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-007-community-membership-eligibility.md) | Business status that recognizes whether an owner may participate in communication for a specific community. | Community participation entitlement, community standing | Use for recognized business entitlement, not technical access control. |
| [`Community wall post`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-008-community-wall-post.md) | Community-specific communication item issued in the governed communication space. | Community announcement, community notice | Use for official or accountable communication items in scope. |
| [`Support ticket`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-009-support-ticket.md) | Accountable business matter raised by an owner to the responsible administrator company. | Support matter, service request | Do not assume the business granularity is fully settled between community and unit level. |
| [`Communication record`](./02-core-business-concepts/CON-010-communication-record.md) | Durable business evidence of a meaningful communication or support interaction. | Business record, communication history | Use when continuity, traceability, or historical truth matters. |

## Usage Boundaries

- Prefer the exact glossary terms above in this chapter and reuse them in later chapters where the same business meaning applies.
- Keep `Community membership eligibility` separate from implementation ideas such as identity, role, or access-control design.
- Keep `Administrator assignment` separate from the administrator company itself so historical and active responsibility can be discussed clearly.
- Keep semantic conflicts visible in `05-semantic-issues-and-open-questions.md` instead of resolving them silently through inconsistent wording.
