# UC-007: Reassign Community To New Administrator Company

## Goal / Outcome

The **Operator** transfers a **Community** from one **Administrator company** to another without creating a gap in accountability or losing historical traceability.

## Primary Business Actor

**Operator**

## Other Participating Actors

- **Community**
- **Administrator company**
- **Owner**

## Related Capabilities

- Primary: `Administrator reassignment governance`
- Supporting: `Community assignment governance`
- Supporting: `Support ticket management`
- Supporting: `Business record stewardship`

## Trigger

The **Operator** decides that responsibility for a **Community** must move from the current **Administrator company** to a new one.

## Preconditions

- The **Community** currently has one active **Administrator assignment**.
- The incoming **Administrator company** has already been onboarded.
- The business can identify the intended change boundary, including enough understanding of open matters and records to govern continuity.

## Postconditions

- The previous active assignment has ended and a new active assignment has begun.
- The business can show who is accountable for the community after the change.
- Historical assignment, communication, and support context remain traceable across the transition.

## Main Success Flow

1. The **Operator** determines that a **Community** must move to a different **Administrator company**.
2. The operator confirms the incoming administrator relationship is eligible to take responsibility.
3. The operator defines the business handover boundary between outgoing and incoming responsibility.
4. The operator determines how open support matters, active communications, and historical records will remain accountable through the change.
5. The outgoing assignment is ended and the incoming assignment becomes the one active **Administrator assignment** for the community.
6. The business preserves the historical trail of the change and the continuity decisions attached to it.

## Alternate Flows

- The community changes administrator companies even though no open support matters exist, so the focus stays on preserving historical record continuity.
- Time-sensitive communications remain active during the transition, so continuity decisions emphasize who remains accountable for those messages after the handover boundary.
- The outgoing administrator retains a narrow residual obligation for previously opened matters while the incoming administrator takes over new responsibility under a governed transition arrangement.

## Exception Flows

- The effective date of the reassignment is disputed, so the business cannot yet determine who is accountable for current communication or support activity.
- Open matters cannot be given a clear continuation path, so reassignment is paused until accountability is clarified.
- The continuity expectations for records, comments, or tickets are too unclear to support a defensible handover.
- The incoming **Administrator company** is not yet in a valid onboarded state, so reassignment cannot proceed.

## Business Rules Involved

- A **Community** must still have exactly one active **Administrator company** at a time during reassignment.
- Historical **Administrator assignments** remain part of the permanent business record.
- Open support and communication matters must not be left without accountable business ownership.
- The dossier does not yet assume that every record type follows the same continuity or transfer rule during administrator change.

## Information Used

- Current and future **Administrator assignment** details
- Community identity and transition timing
- Open **Support tickets**, active announcements, comments, and related **Communication records**
- Historical assignment and accountability records

## KPIs Or Measures Affected

- Share of reassignments completed without an accountability gap
- Completeness of preserved assignment and record history after transition
- Number of open matters with a clearly defined continuation path during reassignment

## Notes And Assumptions

- Reassignment is the chapter's highest-risk business change because governance, communication, support, and record continuity intersect here.
- The exact transfer, retention, and access rules for historical records remain an explicit unresolved business question.
