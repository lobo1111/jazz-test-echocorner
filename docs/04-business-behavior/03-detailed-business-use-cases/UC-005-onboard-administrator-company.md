# UC-005: Onboard Administrator Company

## Goal / Outcome

The **Operator** recognizes an **Administrator company** as an approved participant in the governed portfolio, creating the business basis for future community assignment and oversight.

## Primary Business Actor

**Operator**

## Other Participating Actors

- **Administrator company**

## Related Capabilities

- Primary: `Administrator company onboarding`
- Supporting: `Business record stewardship`

## Trigger

The **Operator** decides to evaluate an **Administrator company** for participation in the governed domain.

## Preconditions

- A candidate **Administrator company** has been identified.
- The **Operator** has authority to admit portfolio participants.
- The business has enough participation criteria to make an onboarding decision, even if some criteria still need later refinement.

## Postconditions

- The candidate is either recognized as an onboarded **Administrator company** or declined with a documented rationale.
- The business has a durable record of the onboarding decision and the basis on which it was made.
- If approved, the administrator company becomes eligible for later community assignment.

## Main Success Flow

1. The **Operator** identifies a candidate **Administrator company** for portfolio participation.
2. The operator gathers the business basis for evaluating the candidate's role, accountability, and fit.
3. The operator reviews whether the candidate can participate under the domain's governance expectations.
4. The operator confirms the terms under which the administrator company will be recognized as a portfolio participant.
5. The candidate is approved as an onboarded **Administrator company**.
6. The onboarding decision and its basis are preserved as part of the portfolio record.

## Alternate Flows

- The operator grants conditional approval while specific business obligations still need to be clarified before any community assignment occurs.
- The onboarding is narrow in scope because the administrator company is being admitted for a limited portfolio segment or pilot context.

## Exception Flows

- The candidate cannot demonstrate the accountability or participation basis expected by the **Operator**, so onboarding is declined.
- The business criteria for participation are too incomplete to support a credible decision, so onboarding is deferred.
- The operator identifies unresolved legal or governance concerns that must be clarified before the administrator company can be admitted.

## Business Rules Involved

- Only onboarded **Administrator companies** may be assigned responsibility for a **Community**.
- The **Operator** remains the governing party for admission into the portfolio.
- Onboarding decisions should create a durable basis for later oversight, assignment, and accountability.

## Information Used

- Candidate **Administrator company** identity and business standing
- Participation expectations and governance obligations
- Onboarding decision rationale and supporting portfolio records

## KPIs Or Measures Affected

- Time to onboard qualified administrator companies
- Share of onboarded participants with complete governance records
- Readiness of onboarded companies for assignment without later clarification gaps

## Notes And Assumptions

- This use case does not define the exact commercial, legal, or compliance evidence required for approval; those details remain subject to later business clarification.
- The dossier treats onboarding as a governance decision, not a technical provisioning step.
