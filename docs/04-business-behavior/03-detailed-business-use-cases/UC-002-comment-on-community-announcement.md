# UC-002: Comment On Community Announcement

## Goal / Outcome

An eligible **Owner** contributes a permitted comment on an official community communication so that clarification, response, or limited discussion can occur within governed business boundaries.

## Primary Business Actor

**Owner**

## Other Participating Actors

- **Administrator company**
- **Community**

## Related Capabilities

- Primary: `Community discussion governance`
- Supporting: `Owner eligibility management`
- Supporting: `Business record stewardship`

## Trigger

An **Owner** wants to respond to, clarify, or question an official community announcement that concerns a specific **Community**.

## Preconditions

- An official community announcement already exists for the relevant **Community**.
- The **Community** has one active **Administrator assignment**.
- The **Owner** has community-specific standing through an **Estate / unit**.
- Commenting is permitted for the type of announcement in question.

## Postconditions

- The comment is recognized as part of the governed discussion around the announcement.
- The business can show which **Owner** participated in which community-specific discussion.
- The resulting exchange remains part of the **Communication record** for later review and continuity needs.

## Main Success Flow

1. The **Owner** identifies an official announcement that concerns a **Community** in which the owner has standing.
2. The owner's standing to participate in that community discussion is confirmed through the relevant **Estate / unit** relationship.
3. The owner frames a comment that stays within the permitted scope of community discussion.
4. The comment is attached to the announcement as part of the governed community exchange.
5. The comment becomes visible within the business context of that announcement and community.
6. The discussion is preserved as part of the ongoing **Communication record**.

## Alternate Flows

- The owner asks for clarification rather than expressing a view, so the comment functions as a request for explanation about the announcement.
- The owner holds multiple **Estates / units** across communities, so the relevant community standing is clarified before the comment is accepted.
- The owner comment identifies a service problem better handled as a support matter, so the interaction is redirected toward a separate support use case.

## Exception Flows

- The owner lacks valid standing in the relevant **Community**, so participation is not permitted.
- The announcement is treated as one-way communication, so no commenting is allowed for that item.
- The proposed comment falls outside the currently confirmed business scope for owner discussion, so it is withheld pending business clarification.
- The active **Administrator assignment** is disputed during a transition period, so accountability for the discussion cannot yet be confirmed.

## Business Rules Involved

- Commenting rights are community-specific and depend on valid owner standing.
- Community discussion is governed interaction, not a general owner-to-owner social space.
- Discussion must remain tied to an official community communication context.
- Comment history should remain part of the community record even if the active administrator relationship later changes.

## Information Used

- Owner standing through **Estate / unit**
- The relevant community announcement
- Community-specific participation rules
- Prior related comments and **Communication records**

## KPIs Or Measures Affected

- Share of comments accepted from correctly entitled owners
- Timeliness of clarification on official communications
- Volume of comments redirected because they fall outside discussion scope

## Notes And Assumptions

- This use case intentionally stays narrow and does not assume a broader owner-to-owner communication model.
- The dossier does not yet decide whether all eligible owners see the same discussion in every case or whether some announcements require narrower participation.
