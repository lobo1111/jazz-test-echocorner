# Behavioral Overview

The business behavior of **Housing Community Administration and Resident Communication in Poland** follows a governed lifecycle rather than an informal communication pattern. The domain begins with portfolio control by the **Operator**, moves into active community responsibility by the assigned **Administrator company**, and then expresses that responsibility through owner-facing communication and support work.

At a high level, the business unfolds across five outcome areas:

1. an **Administrator company** is admitted into the governed portfolio
2. a **Community** is assigned to one active administrator relationship
3. eligible **Owners** receive community-specific announcements and may participate in permitted discussion
4. owners raise support matters and the responsible administrator company manages them to resolution
5. if responsibility changes, the business reassigns the community while preserving continuity and historical traceability

The chapter's use cases represent those outcome areas directly. They show that owner-facing behavior depends on upstream governance. An announcement, comment, or support ticket is never free-floating in this domain; it is tied to community-specific standing, current administrator accountability, and durable business records.

The greatest business complexity concentrates in three places:

- determining whether an owner is entitled to participate in a specific community interaction
- deciding whether a support matter should be treated as community-wide, estate-specific, or unit-specific
- preserving responsibility, open matters, and historical records when one administrator company's assignment ends and another begins

The selected use cases are intentionally compact but representative. Together they cover the core behavior promised by the current dossier scope without drifting into broader social interaction, detailed legal procedure, or implementation design. The scenarios then show how the model behaves when continuity, eligibility, or communication urgency makes business judgment especially important.
