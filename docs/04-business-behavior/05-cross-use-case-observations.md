# Cross-Use-Case Observations

## Recurring Decision Points

Across the chapter, the same business decisions appear repeatedly:

- which **Administrator company** is currently accountable for a **Community**
- whether an **Owner** has standing in that community through an **Estate / unit**
- whether the matter should be treated as community-wide, estate-specific, or unit-specific
- how much of the resulting activity must remain visible as part of the durable business record

These decisions explain why `Community assignment governance`, `Administrator reassignment governance`, `Owner eligibility management`, and `Business record stewardship` shape multiple use cases rather than appearing only once.

## Repeated Exception Themes

The same exception patterns recur across communication, support, and governance work:

- responsibility becomes unclear when reassignment timing is disputed
- broader owner-to-owner interaction exceeds the currently confirmed business scope
- support matters do not fit neatly into either community-level or estate- or unit-level framing
- legal or formal significance of a communication cannot yet be assumed safely

The chapter therefore records ambiguity explicitly instead of smoothing it away.

## Handoff Patterns

The business behavior of the domain depends on several recurring handoffs:

- the **Operator** governs which **Administrator companies** may participate and which communities they are responsible for
- the active **Administrator company** becomes the accountable party for official community communication and support handling
- **Owners** participate only through community-specific standing, not through a general platform membership right
- during reassignment, responsibility must move between administrator relationships without leaving open matters or records unowned

## Rule Hotspots

Some business rules appear so often that they act as control points for the whole chapter:

- a **Community** has exactly one active **Administrator company** at a time
- owner participation is specific to the relevant community and grounded in an **Estate / unit**
- official community communication must remain tied to the current **Administrator assignment**
- assignment, communication, comment, and support history all contribute to the durable business record

## Ambiguous Business Areas

The most important unresolved areas that cut across the chapter are:

- whether **Estate** and **Unit** are interchangeable or distinct business concepts
- whether owner discussion should stay limited to announcement comments or expand further
- whether support scope should be modeled mainly at community level, estate level, unit level, or by case
- how historical announcements, comments, and tickets remain visible or transferable after reassignment
- which communications may carry stronger formal or legal significance in the Polish operating model

## Dossier-Level Observation

This chapter shows that the domain's visible day-to-day behavior depends on upstream governance decisions and stable business vocabulary. If later chapters refine concepts such as **Administrator assignment**, **Community membership eligibility**, **Support ticket**, and **Communication record**, those refinements should clarify the decisions already exposed here rather than replace the behavioral model with a technical one.
