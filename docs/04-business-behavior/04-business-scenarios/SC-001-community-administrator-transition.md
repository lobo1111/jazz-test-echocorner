# SC-001: Community Administrator Transition

## Business Context

A **Community** is changing from one active **Administrator company** to another. The change happens while the community still depends on official communications, ongoing support matters, and preserved historical records.

## Triggering Event

The **Operator** sets in motion a reassignment that will end the current **Administrator assignment** and begin a new one.

## Narrative

The community is already operating under an established administrator relationship when the **Operator** decides that responsibility must move to a different **Administrator company**. This transition is not only a portfolio decision; it also changes who is accountable for owner communication, who must handle open support matters, and how the continuity of community records should be preserved.

Owners still need to understand who is responsible for their community during the transition. Active announcements may still matter, comment histories may still shape community understanding, and unresolved support tickets may still require accountable handling. The business therefore has to define a clear handover boundary and decide how continuity will be preserved across that boundary.

The scenario illustrates that reassignment is not just a new assignment. It is a business event in which historical truth, current accountability, and owner confidence all need protection at the same time.

## Key Decisions

- When does the outgoing **Administrator company** stop being accountable, and when does the incoming one become accountable?
- Which open support matters continue under the incoming relationship, and which remain tied to the outgoing one until closure?
- How should active announcements, comments, and other **Communication records** remain visible or attributable after the transition?
- What must be preserved as part of the permanent historical record of the community?

## Possible Outcomes

- The reassignment completes with a clear accountability boundary and no gap in owner-facing responsibility.
- The transition completes, but some open matters remain under special continuity rules that require explicit governance.
- The reassignment is delayed because record continuity or accountable ownership of open matters cannot yet be clarified.

## Risks, Tensions, Or Observations

- Reassignment concentrates the chapter's highest business risk because governance, communication, support, and records all depend on the change.
- Owners may lose confidence if they cannot tell which **Administrator company** is currently responsible.
- The business does not yet know whether every record type should follow the same transfer, retention, or access rule after a transition.
