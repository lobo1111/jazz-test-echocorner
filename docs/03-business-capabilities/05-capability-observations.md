# Capability Observations

## Concentration Of Business Importance

The capability landscape is most sensitive where responsibility changes hands or where participation rights must be proven. For that reason, `Community assignment governance`, `Administrator reassignment governance`, `Owner eligibility management`, `Support ticket management`, and `Business record stewardship` form the core control layer of the chapter.

## Current Maturity Concerns

- Governance capabilities are conceptually clear, but the business criteria for onboarding, oversight, and reassignment are still incomplete.
- Communication capabilities are understood at a high level, but the allowed depth of owner discussion remains unresolved.
- Support handling is clearly in scope, yet the exact business framing of a support matter still needs sharper definition.
- Record stewardship is essential but under-specified, especially for administrator transitions and historical access expectations.

## Concentrated Pain Points

- Administrator reassignment is the highest-risk capability intersection because assignment, communication, support, and records all depend on it.
- Owner eligibility is likely to be a recurring source of disagreement because participation is community-specific and tied to property relationships.
- Record continuity may become a compliance risk if legal obligations require stronger retention or transfer rules than currently documented.

## Capability Gaps And Likely Follow-Up

- The current map does not yet break down oversight beyond onboarding and reassignment; later analysis may need a sharper view of ongoing administrator-company governance.
- The chapter assumes a controlled communication model. If the business confirms broader owner interaction, `Community discussion governance` may need decomposition.
- The chapter treats support as one capability. If legal or operational distinctions prove significant, separate capabilities may be needed for intake, resolution accountability, and closure assurance.

## Business Implications

If these capabilities are stabilized early, later chapters can describe use cases and concepts without renaming core business abilities. If they remain ambiguous, downstream work is likely to drift into inconsistent language around who is responsible, who may participate, and which records survive business change.
