# In-Scope Capabilities

The following capabilities are the most important to the current dossier scope. They are written as stable business abilities and intentionally avoid process sequencing or implementation detail.

## Administrator company onboarding

- **Definition:** The ability to admit an administrator company into the governed operating portfolio with clear accountability and participation terms.
- **Why it matters:** The domain depends on a controlled population of administrator companies before any community assignment or owner-facing responsibility can exist.
- **Current issue or pain:** The business criteria for participation, oversight, and compliance are not yet fully normalized.
- **Desired outcome:** The business can recognize an administrator company as an authorized participant with a clear basis for future assignment and oversight.
- **Relative importance:** High
- **Likely primary owner:** Operator

## Community assignment governance

- **Definition:** The ability to determine and maintain which administrator company is currently responsible for a community.
- **Why it matters:** The dossier treats exactly one active administrator company per community as a core business invariant.
- **Current issue or pain:** Community responsibility can become ambiguous if assignment rules, accountability, or timing are not explicit.
- **Desired outcome:** Every community has one clear current administrator relationship that can be relied on for communication and support accountability.
- **Relative importance:** Critical
- **Likely primary owner:** Operator

## Administrator reassignment governance

- **Definition:** The ability to transfer community responsibility from one administrator company to another while preserving continuity and historical traceability.
- **Why it matters:** Administrator change is a high-risk business event because responsibility, records, and owner expectations must remain coherent through the transition.
- **Current issue or pain:** Handover rules for announcements, comments, tickets, and historical records remain uncertain and can create business discontinuity.
- **Desired outcome:** Reassignment becomes a controlled business change with explicit historical continuity and no ambiguity about who is currently accountable.
- **Relative importance:** Critical
- **Likely primary owner:** Operator

## Owner eligibility management

- **Definition:** The ability to determine whether an owner is entitled to access and participate in communication for a specific community.
- **Why it matters:** Communication rights in this domain are community-specific and must follow the owner's estate or unit relationship rather than general platform membership.
- **Current issue or pain:** The exact business basis for eligibility may vary across communities and is not yet fully normalized.
- **Desired outcome:** Only eligible owners can participate in the community interactions relevant to their owned estates or units.
- **Relative importance:** Critical
- **Likely primary owner:** Administrator company

## Community announcement management

- **Definition:** The ability to issue and maintain official community communications for the relevant owner audience.
- **Why it matters:** Owners need reliable, community-specific information from the responsible administrator company.
- **Current issue or pain:** Communication scope, formality, and retention duties may differ depending on legal or operational context.
- **Desired outcome:** Communities have a trusted channel for official notices, updates, and service-relevant announcements.
- **Relative importance:** High
- **Likely primary owner:** Administrator company

## Community discussion governance

- **Definition:** The ability to allow and govern owner participation around community communications within approved business boundaries.
- **Why it matters:** The dossier expects at least controlled discussion around community announcements, but the full scope of owner-to-owner interaction remains unresolved.
- **Current issue or pain:** It is not yet confirmed whether discussion stays narrow and comment-based or expands into broader community interaction.
- **Desired outcome:** Owners can participate in permitted discussion without blurring the boundary between governed communication and general social networking.
- **Relative importance:** Medium
- **Likely primary owner:** Administrator company

## Support ticket management

- **Definition:** The ability to receive, manage, and resolve owner-raised support or maintenance matters under the responsibility of the relevant administrator company.
- **Why it matters:** Support handling is one of the clearest day-to-day expressions of administrator accountability to owners.
- **Current issue or pain:** The business has not yet finalized whether tickets are primarily community-level, unit-level, or both depending on the case.
- **Desired outcome:** Owners can raise accountable matters to the correct administrator company and obtain a visible resolution path.
- **Relative importance:** Critical
- **Likely primary owner:** Administrator company

## Business record stewardship

- **Definition:** The ability to preserve the records needed to prove accountability, continuity, and historical truth across assignments, communications, and support matters.
- **Why it matters:** The domain explicitly requires historical administrator assignments to remain part of the business record, and the same continuity problem affects communications and tickets.
- **Current issue or pain:** Retention, transfer, and access expectations for records during administrator change are still unclear.
- **Desired outcome:** The business can rely on durable records even when responsibility moves from one administrator company to another.
- **Relative importance:** Critical
- **Likely primary owner:** Shared between Operator and Administrator company
