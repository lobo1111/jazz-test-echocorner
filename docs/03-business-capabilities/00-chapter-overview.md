# Chapter 2 Overview

This chapter defines the business capabilities for **Housing Community Administration and Resident Communication in Poland**. In this dossier, a capability is a stable business ability that the domain must possess regardless of which organization performs the work or how any future solution is implemented.

## What This Chapter Covers

This chapter shows the business abilities needed to:

- govern administrator companies at portfolio level
- assign and reassign communities to the correct administrator company
- determine which owners are entitled to participate in community communication
- support community announcements, discussion, and owner support requests
- preserve the records and accountability needed across administrator changes

## What This Chapter Does Not Cover

This chapter does not describe:

- business process flows or step-by-step scenarios
- application functions, screens, APIs, or data design
- detailed legal interpretation beyond business constraints already known
- adjacent domains such as billing, general social networking, or full facility operations

## How To Read This Chapter

Read this chapter from overview to detail:

1. `01-capability-view-overview.md` explains how the capability model is organized.
2. `02-capability-map.md` provides the main hierarchy of capability groups and capabilities.
3. `03-in-scope-capabilities.md` expands the capabilities that matter most to the current dossier scope.
4. `04-capability-boundaries.md` clarifies what is excluded, shared, or still ambiguous.
5. `05-capability-observations.md` summarizes the main maturity concerns and business implications.
