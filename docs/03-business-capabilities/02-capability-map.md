# Capability Map

The capability hierarchy below reflects the stable business abilities required for **Housing Community Administration and Resident Communication in Poland**. It emphasizes governance, entitlement, communication, and continuity rather than process sequence.

## Level 1 To Level 2 Hierarchy

### 1. Portfolio Governance

- **Administrator company onboarding**
  - establish that a new administrator company may participate in the governed portfolio
  - confirm the business basis for oversight, accountability, and ongoing participation
- **Community assignment governance**
  - control which administrator company is currently responsible for each community
  - preserve the rule that each community has exactly one active administrator company at a time
- **Administrator reassignment governance**
  - govern the transition of a community from one administrator company to another
  - maintain continuity, accountability, and historical traceability through the change

### 2. Community Participation Management

- **Owner eligibility management**
  - determine which owners are entitled to access and participate in a community's communication space
  - preserve the link between owned estate or unit and community-specific participation rights
- **Community announcement management**
  - create, maintain, and retire official community communications
  - ensure announcements are relevant to the correct community and accountable party
- **Community discussion governance**
  - allow and govern permitted owner participation around community communications
  - keep discussion within the business scope confirmed for the domain

### 3. Service Accountability Management

- **Support ticket management**
  - receive, track, and resolve owner-raised support or maintenance matters
  - keep responsibility aligned with the administrator company serving the relevant community
- **Business record stewardship**
  - preserve assignment, communication, and support records needed for continuity and accountability
  - retain the business history needed across community changes and administrator transitions

## Grouping Rationale

The first group focuses on who is authorized to administer a community. The second focuses on who may participate in community-facing communication and in what form. The third focuses on service obligations and the records that make those obligations auditable over time.

This structure keeps the capability map readable while still making the most important business tensions visible:

- governance versus day-to-day participation
- current responsibility versus historical continuity
- community-specific rights versus broader platform presence
