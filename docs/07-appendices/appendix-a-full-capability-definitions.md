# Appendix A: Full Capability Definitions

This appendix holds the expanded capability catalog for **Housing Community Administration and Resident Communication in Poland**. It complements Chapter 2 by providing fuller definitions without making the main chapter harder to read.

## Level 1 Capability Groups

### Portfolio governance

The business ability to control which administrator companies may participate in the governed domain and which communities they are responsible for over time.

### Community participation management

The business ability to determine who may participate in community-facing communication and to govern the forms of that participation.

### Service accountability management

The business ability to handle owner-raised matters and preserve the records needed to prove continuity and accountability.

## Level 2 Capability Catalog

### Administrator company onboarding

The ability to recognize an administrator company as an approved participant in the operating portfolio. This includes establishing the business basis for future oversight, assignment, and accountability.

### Community assignment governance

The ability to establish and maintain the active responsibility link between a community and its current administrator company. This capability protects the invariant that one community has exactly one active administrator company at a time.

### Administrator reassignment governance

The ability to end one active administrator relationship and replace it with another while preserving continuity of responsibility and historical traceability. This capability is central to the domain because it joins governance, accountability, and record continuity.

### Owner eligibility management

The ability to determine whether an owner is entitled to view or participate in the communications of a specific community. The basis for eligibility is tied to the owner's estate or unit relationship to that community.

### Community announcement management

The ability to issue and maintain official community-facing communications that are relevant to the right owner audience and attributable to the responsible administrator company.

### Community discussion governance

The ability to allow, constrain, and oversee owner participation around community communications. In the current dossier scope, this capability is intentionally narrower than a general social-networking model.

### Support ticket management

The ability to receive, track, and resolve owner-raised support or maintenance matters that fall under the administrator company's responsibility for the relevant community. The exact boundary between community-level and unit-level support remains an open business question.

### Business record stewardship

The ability to preserve the assignment, communication, and support records needed to maintain business continuity, prove accountability, and retain historical truth across administrator changes.
