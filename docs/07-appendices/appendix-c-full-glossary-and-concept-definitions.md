# Appendix C: Full Glossary And Concept Definitions

This appendix provides the extended concept catalog for **Housing Community Administration and Resident Communication in Poland**. It complements Chapter 4 by collecting the main concept definitions in one place and adding longer notes where the chapter-level files intentionally stay concise.

## Extended Concept Catalog

### Operator

The **Operator** is the portfolio-governance party for the domain. It determines which administrator companies may participate, oversees assignment and reassignment decisions, and protects continuity where community responsibility changes over time.

Extended note: the operator may later prove to have stronger compliance, supervisory, or record-governance duties than are currently confirmed. Until that is clarified, the dossier should treat the operator as the main governance authority above individual administrator relationships.

### Administrator Company

An **Administrator company** is the organization that manages one or more communities during the period in which those communities are assigned to it. It is the main operating party for governed community communication, owner-facing support handling, and day-to-day continuity while the assignment remains active.

Extended note: the administrator company should be treated as accountable only within the communities and periods covered by active assignments. Historical records may remain attributable to that company even after responsibility moves elsewhere.

### Community

A **Community** is the enduring housing-community body whose administration, communication, and support matters are being governed. It remains the central subject even when the active administrator company changes.

Extended note: the community should not be reduced to a communication audience. It is the business body whose obligations, records, and owner participation rights make the rest of the concept model meaningful.

### Administrator Assignment

An **Administrator assignment** is the time-bound business relationship that makes one administrator company responsible for one community. It provides the bridge between governance and operations by making current accountability explicit while preserving historical sequence.

Extended note: this is the pivotal concept for the dossier because it explains both present responsibility and continuity through change. Many later questions about posts, tickets, and records become clearer once they are expressed in relation to the assignment period in force.

### Owner

An **Owner** is the person whose standing within a community arises through ownership of one or more estates or units linked to that community. The owner is the main participating party on the community side of the domain.

Extended note: owner participation is not general platform membership. It is community-specific standing that depends on a recognized business basis and may differ from one community to another.

### Estate / Unit

**Estate / unit** is the controlled paired term for the property concept through which an owner's community standing is anchored. The dossier intentionally keeps the paired term because the precise relationship between `estate` and `unit` has not yet been normalized.

Extended note: later refinement may split this into separate concepts if the distinction proves business-significant. Until then, the paired term helps keep the glossary stable without hiding the ambiguity.

### Community Membership Eligibility

**Community membership eligibility** is the business standing that entitles a specific owner to access or participate in communication for a specific community. It is the concept that translates ownership-based standing into governed participation rights.

Extended note: eligibility may not be all-or-nothing. The business may later distinguish between rights to receive announcements, comment on posts, raise support matters, or access historical records.

### Community Wall Post

A **Community wall post** is a governed communication item published for the owner audience of a specific community under the responsibility of the active administrator company. It is the main concept for official or semi-official community-facing communication in the current dossier scope.

Extended note: the dossier uses this term narrowly so it does not drift into unrestricted social-network language. Some posts may later prove to have stronger formal-notice meaning than others.

### Support Ticket

A **Support ticket** is an accountable business matter raised by an owner to the administrator company responsible for the relevant community so that the matter can be tracked and resolved. It captures the service-accountability side of the domain.

Extended note: the business may later need a sharper distinction between community-wide support matters and estate- or unit-specific ones. For now, one concept is sufficient, provided that the ambiguity remains explicit.

### Communication Record

A **Communication record** is the durable business evidence that communication, participation, support, or responsibility events occurred and remain relevant to continuity, accountability, or historical truth.

Extended note: this term is intentionally broader than any one post or ticket. It exists to describe the evidentiary layer that survives current operations and matters especially during reassignment, dispute review, or later business interpretation.

## Cross-Concept Notes

### Time-Bound Responsibility

The most important pattern in the concept model is that responsibility is time-bound. `Administrator assignment` is therefore not a supporting detail; it is the relationship that gives meaning to current accountability and historical continuity across the whole chapter.

### Ownership-Based Participation

Owner participation is anchored through `Estate / unit` and expressed through `Community membership eligibility`. This keeps communication rights tied to business standing rather than to generic platform presence.

### Durable Accountability

`Community wall post`, `Support ticket`, and `Communication record` together describe how business interaction becomes accountable history. This pattern matters because the business story must remain coherent even when an administrator relationship changes.

## Deep Glossary Notes

- Prefer `Administrator assignment` over vague phrases such as "current administrator relationship" when the time-bound nature of responsibility matters.
- Prefer `Community membership eligibility` over generic terms such as "user access" or "member status" because this chapter is business-facing, not technical.
- Prefer `Communication record` when the point is retention, continuity, or evidentiary value rather than the communication event itself.
- Keep `Estate / unit` as a paired expression until the dossier resolves whether those are two concepts or one.
# Appendix C: Full Glossary And Concept Definitions

This appendix expands the Chapter 4 vocabulary with slightly fuller business notes. It is a reference companion to `docs/05-business-information/04-glossary.md`, not a technical model or attribute inventory.

## Extended Concept Catalog

### Operator

The `Operator` is the portfolio-governance party that decides which administrator companies may participate and how community responsibility is governed across the portfolio. It matters most where onboarding, oversight, assignment, or reassignment decisions affect continuity and accountability.

### Administrator Company

An `Administrator company` is the organization that carries day-to-day administrative responsibility for communities during active assignments. Its business significance comes from the fact that communication, support handling, and record stewardship depend on which company is currently responsible for a community.

### Community

A `Community` is the enduring housing-community body at the center of the domain. It is not merely the current audience for a message stream; it is the business subject whose administration, communication, support matters, and records continue over time even when administrator responsibility changes.

### Administrator Assignment

An `Administrator assignment` is the business relationship that connects one administrator company to one community for a defined period of responsibility. This concept is essential because it expresses both the current administrator invariant and the historical sequence of changes that later chapters must preserve.

### Owner

An `Owner` is the person whose standing within a community arises through owned estates or units. The concept matters because owner participation, visibility of communication, and the right to raise support matters depend on legitimate standing rather than general user presence.

### Estate / Unit

`Estate / unit` is the controlled paired term for the property concept that anchors an owner's standing. The paired wording is intentional because the dossier has not yet normalized whether estate and unit are equivalent or distinct business concepts in the Polish operating context.

### Community Membership Eligibility

`Community membership eligibility` is the recognized business status that determines whether a specific owner may participate in communication for a specific community. It is the bridge between property-based standing and controlled participation rights.

### Community Wall Post

A `Community wall post` is the accountable communication item used for community-specific announcements, notices, or updates in scope for this dossier. It gives the business a stable term for official community-facing communication without assuming a broader social interaction model.

### Support Ticket

A `Support ticket` is the accountable business matter raised by an owner to the administrator company responsible for the relevant community. It creates a durable object around issue handling, progress, and resolution rather than treating support as informal correspondence.

### Communication Record

A `Communication record` is the durable business evidence of meaningful communication or support interaction, including the historical context needed to interpret responsibility later. The concept matters most during reassignment, dispute, audit, or later review.

## Cross-Concept Notes

- `Administrator assignment` is the key continuity concept because it connects governance, responsibility, and historical change.
- `Community membership eligibility` is the key participation concept because it connects owners to a community through recognized standing.
- `Communication record` is the key evidentiary concept because it preserves business meaning across time and administrator change.

## Open Vocabulary Notes

- Keep using `Estate / unit` as a paired term until the business confirms whether the two words are interchangeable.
- Keep treating communication rights as community-specific business entitlements rather than as general platform roles.
- Keep treating support tickets and communication records as business concepts that may survive reassignment, even though the exact retention and transfer obligations remain unresolved.
